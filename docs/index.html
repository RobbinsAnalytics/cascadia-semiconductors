    Data & method

    • Source. SEC EDGAR XBRL APIs (companyfacts), FormFactor CIK 0001039399. Data was pulled once and frozen in the repo (data/raw/); this page renders that snapshot and makes no SEC calls at runtime.
    • GAAP basis. All figures are GAAP as tagged in XBRL filings. FormFactor's press-release headline margins are typically non-GAAP and will not match — e.g. Q1 FY2026 GAAP gross margin here is ~38.4% vs a ~49% non-GAAP headline.
    • Derived Q4 (‡). Income-statement concepts usually lack a discrete Q4 filing (the 10-K reports the full year), so Q4 = FY − (Q1+Q2+Q3), flagged derived in the data. Derived Q4 diluted EPS is approximate: per-share amounts are not strictly additive when share counts move between quarters.
    • Divestiture gains. GAAP operating income includes gains on business sales that sit outside operating expenses: +$72.9M in FY2023 Q4 (FRT Metrology sale) and +$20.3M in FY2024 Q1 (China business sale). Those quarters' operating-income spikes are real GAAP results, not margin improvements.
    • Fiscal calendar. FormFactor uses a 52/53-week fiscal year; each fiscal quarter is mapped to the calendar quarter whose end date is nearest (always within ~6 days). Rule documented in conformance_rules.md.
    • Governance. XBRL tags per metric: tag_mapping.csv · automated reconciliation: validation_report.md. Restatements keep the latest filed value and are flagged. Missing data is flagged, never filled.
