    Governance — how each number is sourced

    The point of this project: cross-company GAAP comparison is only as honest as the XBRL behind it. This is the actual us-gaap tag that fed each company × concept — including where a peer tags a concept differently, or not at all. Nothing is estimated; gaps are shown as gaps.

    Data & method

    • Source & freeze. FormFactor is the frozen Phase 1 snapshot (SEC companyfacts, as-of 2026-07-21). The six peer companyfacts bundles were pulled by src/ingest_peers.py as an additive extension of that freeze (CIKs resolved from the committed SEC ticker map, never guessed); the actual retrieval date is stamped above and in the validation report. The benchmark never shows a peer quarter later than FormFactor's frozen latest, so the slightly later pull cannot leak newer data. The page makes no SEC calls at runtime.
    • GAAP basis & metrics. Revenue, gross margin %, operating margin %, R&D as % of revenue, and YoY revenue growth — all GAAP as tagged in XBRL. Margins are computed from filed GAAP line items, never from non-GAAP headlines.
    • Tag drift, handled by a priority map. Peers tag the same concept differently — e.g. Teradyne's R&D uses ResearchAndDevelopmentExpenseExcludingAcquiredInProcessCost; Cohu and inTEST tag revenue as …IncludingAssessedTax. A per-metric tag-priority list absorbs this; the winning tag per company is shown in the table above.
    • Not comparable — flagged, never filled. Camtek is a foreign private issuer filing Form 20-F/6-K, not 10-Q/10-K, so it has no recent quarterly GAAP XBRL and is excluded from the quarterly rankings. KLA tagged GrossProfit only through FY2021 Q2 (it appears in the gross-margin trend through 2021, then stops) and does not tag OperatingIncomeLoss in-window (latest 2015), so its gross and operating margins are not comparable at the snapshot quarter — KLA is shown on revenue scale, R&D %, and growth only. Cohu does not tag GrossProfit at all in-window, so its gross margin is not comparable. These gaps are shown as gaps, not estimated.
    • Fiscal-calendar conformance. Each company's fiscal quarter is mapped to the calendar quarter whose end date is nearest (within ~14 days) — the same rule used for FormFactor — so companies with different fiscal years line up on one grid. Derived Q4 = FY − (Q1+Q2+Q3), flagged; restatements keep the latest filed value.
    • Honest peer-set caveat.
    • Governance artifacts. tag_mapping.csv · validation_report.md (per-peer FY reconciliation, gap audit, and the snapshot-alignment note).
